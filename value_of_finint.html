Three banking theories

Intermediation

Theory 2: money center

Theory 3: asymmetry

Transactions costs

  • search costs to find borrowers & lenders
  • contract costs
  • economies of scale

Risk sharing

  • Intermediaries are experts at bearing risk
  • Asset transformation

Asymmetric Information

  • one party has more info than the other
  • creates problems BEFORE loan is made
  • creates problems AFTER loan is made
  • financial intermediaries minimize the gap

Adverse selection

  • BEFORE the loan
  • people with worst credit are more likely to seek a loan
  • lending not attractive

solution?

  • banks expert at assessing credit risks

Moral hazard

  • AFTER the loan
  • once money is lent, borrower may blow the money

solution?

  • banks monitor borrowers and enforce lending contracts

Policy conduit

  • Channel to implement a variety of policies
  • Demand side policies
  • Supply side policies

Types of intermediaries

Depository institutions

“banks” accept deposits, make loans

Commercial Banks

  • Asset Transformation
  • Information Assymetry
  • Economies of scale

Savings & Loans

  • originally restricting to savings deposits and mortgages
  • less restricted today

Credit Unions

  • consumer loans
  • nonprofit
  • organized around a group

Finance companies

  • issue commercial paper
  • consumer & commercial loans

Contractual savings / insurance

acquire funds through payments in return for obligations

life insurance

  • fixed serial payments for initial period (say 20 years)
  • fixed serial repayments for final period (say 15 years)
  • Similar to Pension Fund
  • Offers annuity against savings
  • Long-term investment horizon

property and casualty insurance

  • fixed serial payments per period
  • repayment in case of an event
  • Insures clients against certain events for premiums
  • Based on theorem of large numbers
  • Some statistic (actuarial) rules
  • Shorter time horizon

pension funds

  • same as life insurance
  • Mutual Funds for Pensions
  • Defined Benefit
  • Defined Contribution

Investment Intermediaries

  • indirect investments, managed by specialists
  • company balance sheet is separate from the managed assets

Mutual funds

  • sell shares to stock/bond portfolios
  • taken away bank business in past 30 years

NBFI

  • Savings and Loans
  • Credit Unions
  • Other restricted banking license

Brokerages

  • Help to Issue securities
  • Make the market
  • Do not take long-term exposures
  • Pass risk to clients

Mutual Funds

  • Assets are financial instruments
  • Equity is investor money
  • May or may not have debt
  • Managed by separate company

Endowments

  • Funds set for specific purpose
  • Have to spend at least 3% of value annually
  • May or may not have tax advantages
  • Long-Term horizon

Foundations

  • Similar to Endowments
  • Does not have spending requirement
  • Usually long-term horizon

Hedge Funds

  • Investment companies focused at implementing specific strategies
  • Usually risk takers
  • Not always liquid
  • Variety of time horizons
  • Very hard to generalise

Private Equity

  • Buy bad company
  • Make it better
  • Liquidate
  • LBO
  • MBO
  • Presumed expertise
  • Illiquid assets
  • Lock-up period

Venture Capital

  • Risky investments in technology
  • Expertise is essential
  • Illiquid
  • Lock-Up period

An Overview of the Financial System

Debt vs. Equity Markets

debt security

  • cash flows are fixed
  • bonds, loans

equity security

  • cash flow variable, residual
  • common stock

Primary vs. Secondary Markets

primary market

  • newly issued securities / investment banking

secondary market

  • brokers match buyers and sellers
  • dealers act as buyers and sellers
  • market-makers

Exchanges vs. OTC Markets

  • exchange
  • buying & selling of securities in central location with established rules
  • NYSE
  • OTC (over-the-counter)
  • dealers in many locations buy & sell securities

Money vs. Capital Markets

  • money market
  • short-term debt securities (up to 1 yr.)
  • highly liquid, low risk
  • capital market
  • longer-term debt
  • equity

Financial market instruments

a security or financial instrument

claim on future cash flows or assets of issuer

  • a security is an asset for the buyer
  • and a liability for the issuer

Example

  • shares of stock in Armenian Development Bank
  • shares of ownership in ADB
  • a claim on the earnings/assets of ADB
  • a liability for ADB
  • an asset for the owner
